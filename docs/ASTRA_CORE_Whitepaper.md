# 2. Problem Statement  

## 2.1 Fragmentation of Critical Systems  

Modern global systems operate in structural isolation:

- Cybersecurity frameworks lack integration with economic risk models  
- AI governance operates independently of legal and sovereign structures  
- Economic policy is detached from real-time systemic risk signals  
- Defense decisions lack unified predictive validation  

This fragmentation produces systemic blind spots.

---

## 2.2 Absence of Decision Validation Infrastructure  

There exists no globally accepted mechanism to determine:

- Structural stability of policy decisions  
- Cascading systemic risk from strategic actions  
- Alignment of AI-driven actions with sovereign control constraints  

As a result, high-impact decisions are executed under uncertainty, often leading to unintended systemic consequences.

---

## 2.3 Escalation of Global Risk Complexity  

The convergence of the following domains has exponentially increased systemic risk:

- Artificial Intelligence autonomy  
- Cyber warfare and digital infrastructure attacks  
- Financial system interdependence  
- Geopolitical multipolarity  

Without a unified evaluation framework, risk remains partially observable and largely uncontrolled.
